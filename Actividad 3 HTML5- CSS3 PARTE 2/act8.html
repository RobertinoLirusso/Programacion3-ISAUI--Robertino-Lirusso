Old Trafford

Also known as the Theatre of Dreams, Manchester United now uses this stadium as their home ground. Young footballers always look for a chance to play at Old Trafford. Besides hosting the matches of one of the richest football clubs, it now holds the third place in the list of the best football stadiums in the world. Read More

Allianz Arena

This is the third-largest stadium in Germany and can hold up to 70,000 spectators. The stadium gives an amazing look when lit at night. Besides, this one now holds the second place among the best football stadiums in the world. Read More

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

The Santiago Bernabéu is home to one of the most successful clubs in Europe. With nine European titles under its belt, Real Madrid’s place in the upper echelons of world football is assured, and there couldn’t be a more suitable place for a club of this type to play. The Santiago Bernabéu, inaugurated in 1947, has been renovated twice, in 1982 and 2001, to respond to the ambitions of the galacticos. In fact, a new expansion of the stadium is being carried out in order to be able to incorporate a retractable roof. Read More

Wembley Stadium

The Wembley Stadium is undoubtedly one of the most iconic stadiums in world football. Reopened in 2007, the new Wembley was built on the site of the previous Wembley Stadium from 1923. Famous for being one of the most electric atmospheres in world football, the new design has embraced everything that was great about the original stadium and expanded it even further. The famous Twin Towers may no longer stand, but in their place is Wembley Arch. The celebration of some of the most prestigious events in European and international football is now common at “La Casa del Fútbol”. Read More
